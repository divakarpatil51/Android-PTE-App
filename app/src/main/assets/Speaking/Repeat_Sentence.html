Task

After listening to a recording of a sentence, repeat the sentence.

Skills assessed

Listening and speaking

Prompt length

3-9 seconds

Time to answer

15 seconds

This is a short-answer item type that integrates listening and speaking skills, and requires you to understand and remember a sentence, and repeat it using the same words and pronunciation.

You will have 3 seconds for preparation

There are around 10-12 sentences. A single sentence is of around 5 seconds

For more tips go here

Strategy:

  1. Try to understand the sentence. To do so, you need to train your short-term memory or working memory.
  2. Pay attention to the sentence structure, e.g., if the sentence starts with there or it, or if the verb is in active or passive voice. Knowledge of the word order of the main sentence structures will help you rebuild the sentence correctly without leaving out words or adding in the wrong words.
  3. Pronounce all consonants and vowels clearly, and place word and sentence stress correctly. Remember that in English the sentence stress is usually on the last content word or important words.

This item type contributes to following:

  1. Listening
  2. Speaking
  3. Grammar

Scoring:

  Content: 0 - 3 points

  Pronunciation (search on internet)

  Oral fluency

Technique:

This item type requires you to have a good working memory. It holds a small amount of information (typically around 7 items or even less) in mind in an active, readily-available state for a short period of time (typically from 10 to 15 seconds, or sometimes up to a minute). But for PTE, you need to remember around 13-15 items for this item type. So you need to improve your memory by practicing

Step 1: Listening(Understand; don't memorise)

  1. Divide sentence into parts. For eg. The first step / in the application process / is to find / a suitable program.
  2. Remember PHRASES not words.

Step 2: Speaking

    MEAN what you say.: You should repeat the sentence in a meaningful way.

Avoid following things:

  1. No hesitation - no "ummm's" or "ahhhh'ss"
  2. No False start
  3. No repetitions
